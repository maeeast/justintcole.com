Explanation of Roles
  • Actor:

    • Responsible for learning and executing applicable stage directions and reciting lines on cue.

  • Assistant Director:

    • Works with the show Director to create a compelling performance by the actors.

  • Blood Technician:

    • Created a special effects rig that allowed an actress to bleed out through slit wrists on command per the script.

  • Chief:

    • Indicates management of the crew for that show.

  • House Designer:

    • The primary venue of WPI Masque at this time (Alden Hall) had no fixed seating. Each show had to have a seating design created and assembled to ensure the best fit for the audience for the show.

  • Master Carpenter:

    • Responsible, without faculty oversight, for the timely construction and safety of the set.

    • Required budget preparation and adherence as well as time management and worker management skills.

    • Also responsible for striking the entire set and the state of the scene shop and WPI Masque inventory at the end of the show.

  • Mentor:

    • Made expertise available to another undergraduate student learning the task being mentored.

  • Running Crew:

    • Responsible for the silent operation of all backstage operations during a show as well as all scene and set changes on the stage.

  • Senior:

    • Indicates that there were multiple people due to the size of the show and/or the needs of the show.

  • Set Construction Crew:

    • Under the direction of the Master Carpenter this crew builds the set within the specified schedule.

  • Set Designer:

    • Every set requires a design that is informed by the needs outlined by the script. The Set Designer ensures that the design provided to the Master Carpenter meets the needs of the show and carries all of the necessary aesthetic facets to enhance the show.

  • Stage Manager:

    • Responsible for managing and directing the Running Crew and actors during the show.

  • Strike Team:

    • A team of people specifically brought in to demolish a set at the end of a show run.

  • Vice President, Master Carpenter:

    • An elected position that is ultimately responsible for the security and inventory of the scene shop and all of its components as well as providing assistance to the Master Carpenters of each show as needed.
